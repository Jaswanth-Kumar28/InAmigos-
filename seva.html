Project Seva

Project Seva is InAmigos Foundation's primary outreach and emergency relief division. It addresses the basic human needs of hunger, clothing, and shelter for individuals in extremely marginalized, low-income, and disaster-affected communities. By mobilizing teams of young change-makers, we run continuous street-level distribution and support drives.

Core Objectives

Poverty, food insecurity, and extreme weather pose daily threats to millions of daily wage earners and homeless populations. Project Seva serves as a direct aid delivery mechanism to provide immediate relief and long-term dignity to these vulnerable individuals.

🍲 Food & Ration Distribution

Sourcing and serving fresh, healthy meals and supplying monthly dry grocery kits containing grain, pulses, spices, and cooking oil.

👕 Clothes & Winter Relief

Organizing mass collection drives and sharing warm clothes, blankets, shoes, and socks to support street families during winter months.

💊 Hygiene & First Aid

Distributing basic medical supplies, sanitizers, soaps, masks, and sanitary pads to encourage safety and well-being in slums.

🚨 Disaster Outreach Response

Quickly mobilizing resources to provide clean drinking water, biscuits, and emergency aid during local climate crises and heavy rainfalls.

How Interns and Volunteers Contribute

Direct community support requires hands-on logistics and passionate teamwork. Here is how you can support Project Seva:

  • Map out clusters and slums in your locality that need urgent support.
  • Organize small-scale clothes and food collection campaigns with neighbors.
  • Help prepare and pack nutrition kits, sanitizing supplies, or emergency bags.
  • Coordinate weekly distribution runs and lead educational cleanliness sessions.
